The site will bring pertinent current and past data to business owners in order to allow them to make the best use of their time and their employees time. The site itself will make money by marketing in the side panels for snow removal equipment manufacturers.

For example, a certain part of a town may be predicted to have 2in while another section, possible north of a river, will be predicted to have 5 but at a later time than the southern part of town. This allows snow removal companies to decide when to start where especially when compared to the historical data available(i.e. they know it took them 2 hours last year to do a parking lot when it snowed 5in).

The website could be set up in a way that it sends emails or text alerts when new information regarding a client specified area is acquired. Such as snowfall amounts in a city crossing a client determined limit.

Perhaps another feature could be a year end review of total snow fall in selected areas. Predicted future snowfall, etc.

Personas

Persona 1: Sam

Sam owns several contracts with large business that need the be carefully maintained every time it snows. He is legally liable for any negligence in terms of snow removal. He accomplishes this by running several teams and occasionally he himself plows. In order to make the best use of his time and his employees time, he uses Snowplower's Almanac.

Persona 2: Sue

Sue is new to snow plowing and she only has one contract. Hopefully, with the help of her truck and the Snowplower's Almanac she can do well and get some referrals.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: Unexpected Storm

Sam's phone buzzes at 1AM. He reaches to his beside table to see that he as an alert about a surprise storm and the impending snowfall. He had checked the weather before he went to bed, but the weather isn't always easily predicted. Thanks to his subscriptiom he still has time to get his teams ready and his customers wil still be happy.

Scenario 2: No call, No Show

Part of being the boss means you get to solve the big problems. Sam just got a call from one of his team's managers. Apparently, some employees didn't show up. Sam can't afford to wait and see if those employees will wake up so he takes the routes himself. There is only one problem. The routes are on separate ends of the county and the stores open up at nearly the same time. By using his phone and looking at s nowplowersalmanac.com he can see which areas are getting more snow. So, he'll start there first and then go to the second location.

Scenario 3: When to Start?

Sue has finally saved up enough money to buy a good truck and snowplow, but she's not sure when to start. The season's first snowfall is tonight. If she starts too early there might be more snow after she's done and she might have to plow again(not bad moneywise, but she's got school in the morning.) If she starts too late, the store will open and the parking lot won't be finished. Through the website she is able to set an alert that will be sent to her when a total snowfall accumulation of her choice is reached. This way if the snowfall is heavier she will wake up sooner. Otherwise she will get up when her alarm goes off.

Scenario 4: Time for More?

Sue has just finished her route for the day. However, she gets a call from someone asking if she's available to pick up a new route for the rest of the season. By looking at past snowfall amounts from the previous years, Sue estimates that she will still be able to balance her work and school life, although it might be pushing it. She decides that the cash is worth the risk and pickups the route for the rest of the season knowing that her flexibility has probably won her a new client for life.

Scenario 5: Effective and Effecient

Sam is preparing for the next season of snow. He runs 9 teams across 3 cities. Typically, he has kept 3 teams in each city. However, one of the cities he works always takes longer to finish. He is wondering if he can pull some workers from the other teams and create a 10th team. He doesn't want to go about this decision lightly because he knows that creating this team may shake things up a bit in his company. The resistance to change is always strong once people get settled and if he changes his mind down the road his employees might resent him for being fickle or indecisive. After consulting the Snowplower's Almanac, he feels that the data (past snowfall amounts) supports his decision to create another snow removal team.
